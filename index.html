Empowering Humanity Through Hinduism

The Akhil Bharatiya Hindu Mahasabha, traces its roots to the late 19th and early 20th centuries, when various Hindu Sabhas and reform movements arose in North India to safeguard Hindu interests and culture. The first such Hindu Sabha was formed in Lahore in 1906, followed by the Punjab Hindu Sabha in 1909, championed by leaders like Lala Lajpat Rai. Building on these regional initiatives, the All-India (Sarvadeshak) Hindu Sabha was formally established in April 1915 at Haridwar during the Kumbh Mela, under the leadership of Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, with the aim of uniting Hindus politically and socially in the face of colonial reforms and communal challenges. In 1921, the organisation was renamed the Akhil Bharatiya Hindu Mahasabha, which went on to play a prominent role in the nationalist movement, Hindu consolidation, and the articulation of the ideology of Hindutva.
